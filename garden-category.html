Causes

Industrial Waste

Industrial water pollution refers to the contamination of water bodies such as rivers, lakes, and oceans, by various harmful substances produced by industries. Industries such as manufacturing, mining, and chemical production are among the biggest contributors to industrial water pollution.

Causes

Oil Spills

Oil spills can also release toxic chemicals into the water, including polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) and benzene. These chemicals can be absorbed by fish and other wildlife, and can cause reproductive and developmental problems, cancer, and other health issues in humans who consume contaminated fish or water.

Agricultural Activites

Farmers use fertilizers and pesticides to increase crop yield and protect against pests and diseases. Livestock production, including dairy, beef, and poultry farming, can contribute to water pollution through animal waste . Changes in land use, such as deforestation, can alter the natural hydrology of a region and increase runoff and erosion. This can carry pollutants into water bodies and reduce water quality.

Sewage and Wastewater

Sewage and wastewater are terms used to describe the water that is discharged from homes, businesses, and industries after it has been used for various purposes such as bathing, cleaning, and flushing toilets. Sewage typically refers to the wastewater that contains human and animal waste, while wastewater is a broader term that includes sewage as well as other types of used water, such as from washing machines, dishwashers, and industrial processes

Causes

Plastic Waste

Plastic waste refers to the accumulation of discarded plastic products, such as bottles, bags, and packaging, that end up in the environment instead of being properly recycled or disposed of. Plastic waste is a significant environmental problem because plastic products can take hundreds of years to degrade, leading to persistent pollution and harm to wildlife.
